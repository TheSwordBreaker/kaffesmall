What Is A Coffee Bloom?
A bloom coffee occurs when hot water comes in contact with coffee grounds, releasing carbon dioxide and causing the mixture to expand and bubble up. The production of CO2 is a good sign since it indicates that your Kiosk Kaffee is fresh; without carbon dioxide, coffee beans oxidise and become stale.

Full bloom coffee can occur with any brewing method, from traditional drip machines to pour overs. For obvious reasons, you’ll be able to view it best if you visit Kiosk Kaffee and experience a luxurious brewlicious experience in town handcrafted with the classic or best coffee drip machines in the world!

Kiosk Kaffee masters reveal when employing a pour over brew method, lean over, get your nose close to the coffee grounds, and enjoy the fragrances your coffee emits. Professional cuppers at Kiosk Kaffee always smell the coffee as it blooms, and the scent of the bloom can be an excellent indicator of the flavours you’ll taste once it’s ready to serve.

What Is My Coffee Doesn’t Bloom At All?
Freshly ground coffee at Kiosk Kaffee degasses faster, increasing the likelihood of oxidation and decreasing the likelihood of your coffee blooming. There are two techniques to avoid oxidation:

1. Always buy freshly ground coffee beans.

2. Place the beans in an airtight container or bag with a one-way valve which pushes extra air and moisture out of the bag to conserve CO2 and, eventually, shelf life.

Kiosk Expert Tips: It’s crucial to remember that the lack of a coffee bloom does not necessarily imply a terrible cup of coffee; a bloom coffee is merely one of many ways to determine the freshness and quality of your beans. Keep in mind that a coffee’s roast degree and varietal might determine how much it blossoms; for example, dark roasts degas faster than light roasts.

Factors That Affect Coffee Bloom

Temperature
Gas will escape faster from the coffee beans if they are at higher temperatures. Because of this, store your beans in a cool, dry place. Avoid exposing them to prolonged and excessive heat to prevent gases from escaping.

Type of Roast
The type of roast has a huge impact on the quality of the coffee bloom. For instance, longer darker roasts degas to a lesser degree. This is why darker Italian roasts undergo less degassing than other varieties.

Humidity Levels
If coffee beans are stored in a low-humidity environment, more gas can escape. On the other hand, excessive moisture can lead to mould and fungus growth. That is why roasted coffee beans must be stored in a dry, dark environment.

The coffee aroma is just as important as the taste and mouthfeel in pairing it with the perfect dessert. If the coffee has no distinct coffee aroma, it is likely to lack flavour as well. The light roast has the lightest body and the highest acidity of the three roasts. It tastes smooth and mellow. Cookies, fresh fruit tarts, meringues, and fluffy cakes go well with light roasted coffee. The citrus flavours in the coffee beans are brought out by the short roasting time.
